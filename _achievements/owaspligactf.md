# Challenge Creator: OWASP Liga CTF

## Result

I was selected as a challenge creator for OWASP Liga CTF, where I contributed original cybersecurity challenges for participants to solve during the competition.

This role required me to design challenges that were technically reliable, appropriately difficult, and suitable for a competitive CTF environment.

## My contribution

I was responsible for planning, developing, testing, and preparing cybersecurity challenges for the event.

My work involved:

- Designing original challenge concepts
- Defining the intended solution path
- Building the challenge files or environment
- Creating realistic clues and supporting artifacts
- Testing challenges for unintended shortcuts
- Adjusting the difficulty level
- Preparing challenge descriptions and flags
- Verifying that challenges worked correctly before deployment
- Supporting troubleshooting and validation when required

I applied my experience in CTF challenge development across areas such as:

- Digital forensics
- Miscellaneous challenges
- Reverse engineering
- Interactive security puzzles
- File and artifact analysis
- Game-based cybersecurity challenges

I also considered how participants might approach the challenge using automated tools or AI agents, and worked to make the solving process require genuine investigation and interaction.

## Why it matters

Being selected as a challenge creator for an OWASP-affiliated CTF was an important recognition of my skills in cybersecurity challenge design.

Creating a challenge for other participants is different from solving one. It requires understanding the technical topic deeply enough to build a fair problem, predict unintended solutions, and ensure that the challenge remains stable during the competition.

This experience strengthened my skills in:

- Cybersecurity problem design
- Technical testing
- Creative thinking
- Challenge documentation
- Quality assurance
- Adversarial thinking
- Competition preparation

It also gave me further experience contributing to cybersecurity education by creating practical learning experiences for other participants.
